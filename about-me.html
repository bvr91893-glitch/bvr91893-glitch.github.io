About Brian V. Roache

My name is Brian V. Roache, spelled R-O-A-C-H-E. I design and consult with accessibility at the center.

My Story

My name is Brian V. Roache, spelled R-O-A-C-H-E. I am a male student who is blind and has cerebral palsy. I also use a medical walking boot on my right leg.

Accessibility is not just a feature; it is what makes me, me. My life and education have taught me that access should be part of the foundation, not something added later.

Accessibility Philosophy

When I design web pages, I always keep accessibility in mind. My motto is "practicality over pretty," because a website should not only look good; it should work well for real people.

I believe accessible design should be clear, dependable, and usable with assistive technology. A page should make sense to someone using a screen reader, keyboard, magnification, or any practical access tool that helps them participate fully.

Experience

I have worked with organizations ranging from companies like Fidelity to small nonprofits, helping make accessibility work not just for some people, but for everyone.

I enjoy helping people find accessible options in areas like education, banking, technology, and everyday life.

Technical Background

I have experience with Linux, macOS, and Windows, including Windows XP through Windows 11. I enjoy exploring operating systems and accessibility tools because they help users find what works best for them.
