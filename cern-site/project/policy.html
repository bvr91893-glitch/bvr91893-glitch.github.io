Project Policy

The WorldWideWeb project was built around open sharing of information, interoperability, and simple standards that allowed many systems to participate.

  • Information should be easy to publish and easy to find.
  • Documents should link across systems instead of being trapped in silos.
  • Protocols and formats should stay simple enough to be widely adopted.

These ideas helped the web grow beyond CERN into a broadly shared information system.
